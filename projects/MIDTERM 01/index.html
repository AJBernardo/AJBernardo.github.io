WHAT IS A CHESS OPENING?

A chess opening is the initial phase of a chess game, typically encompassing the first 10-15 moves. It focuses on developing pieces, controlling the center of the board, ensuring king safety (often through castling), and preparing for a strong middlegame. Effective openings aim to achieve a harmonious position, minimize weaknesses, and create opportunities for both attack and defense. There are thousands of recognized opening variations, each with unique strategic ideas and plans.
